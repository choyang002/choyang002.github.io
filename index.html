About Me

Before entering Nanyang Technological University (NTU) Singapore, I did a Data Science internship at Grab through my past programming experiences, community involvements, and entrepreneurial endeavors which resulted in me attaining the Harvard Book Award in 2021. I believe I also am quite good at western Chess and I sometimes play it informally online at the Chess Master level as a casual way to test and warm up my mind.


My 3 proudest achievements during my Undergraduate time in NTU Singapore (in no particular order):

— AI/ML research at National Cancer Centre Singapore. I was supervised by Professor Cai Yiyu, Dr Nei Wen Long and Dr Tan Hong Qi. I believed I did very well in this research as I got Top 3 in NTU for my oral research presentation, participated in the International Conference of Undergraduate Research (ICUR) and more suprisingly, I also got commended by top scientists in the field, from UC Berkeley, authors and inventors of CycleGAN, a well known AI technology.

— Pure Mathematics research on Real Analysis and Economics, supervised by Dr Fedor Duzhin. This was intellectually very challenging but I had many interesting conversations with my supervisor and I learnt from Dr Fedor Duzhin how difficult concepts can be taught in more intuitive ways.

— Teaching stints during university to teach students in Singapore neighborhoods from primary school to secondary school how Programming, Mathematics, and Science can be learnt. This taught me empathy, and many other revelations about my own personal character development as well as revealed to me my interest in teaching and reminded me of my past experiences teaching Python to underpriviledged kids at Google's Code In The Community in 2016.
